Question[NOUN] は　[when/who/what]　が　いいですか

Answer[NOUN] いいです

EX1: た(食)べもの　は　なに(何)か　いいですか。ピーザ　が　いいですWhat would you like to eat? I would like pizza.

EX2: パーテｲ　は　なんじ　が　いいですかWhat time would you like to start the party?

EX3 Q: なに(何)か　おみやげを　か(買)いたいです。何が　いいですか。I would like to buy somthing for a souvenir. What to you recommend?

EX3 A: マウスパッドは　どうですか。とても　べんりですよHow about a mouse pad? Very convenient.

Comparisons

EX1: オーストラリア　は　にほん(日本) より　おお(大)きいですAustralia is bigger than Japan

EX2: おおさか　は　おきなわ　より　にんき(人気)　が　ありましosaka is more popular than okinawa

Preference between 2 things

[A]　と　[B]　と　どちら　(が[ADJ]/を[VERB]) [XXX] です[ADJ]/ます[VERB] か。Which do you [XXX] better. [A] or [B]?

EX1 Q: ビールとワインと　どちらが　すきですか Which do you like better, beer or wine?

EX1 A: ビール　のほうが　(ワインより)　好きです。 I like beer better (than wine) - The text between paranthesis is optional

EX2 Q: Youtubeとテレビと　どちらを　よく　み(見)ますか。 Which do you watch more often, Youtube or TV?

EX2 A: youtube のほうが (テレビより)　よく　見ます。 I watch Youtube more often (than TV) - The text between paranthesis is optional

Expressing a sequence of events

Before...

まえ(前)にmeans "before". The verb coming before まえ(前)に is always in the dictionary form regardless of the tense of the verb at the end of the sentence.

EX1: 日本に　くる　前に、日本ご(語)の　べんきょうを　はじめました。I began my studies of Japanese before I came to Japan.

However if the emphasis in on the duration of the previous action, you often omit に.

EX2: 日本に　くる　前、２年[ねん]間[かん]　日本語[ご]の　べんきょうを　していました。I had studied Japanese for 2 years before I came to Japan.
